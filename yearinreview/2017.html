01
01 SCREENMEN
For decades, men live shorter and have higher morbidity and mortality compared to women. Developed based on the user-centered approach, the ScreenMen web contains a short educational video which addresses misconceptions about health screening in men. It can also assess users’ health, provide advice about their health status and what screening tests to go for. Users may also find out some answers regarding screening in the FAQ section. The web also has a function to help users set up and remind them about their next screening date.
Your personal health screening advisor
View Case Study
By 2020, 1 in 6 Malaysians (4.6 Million) will be over the age of 60. However, the general impression of nursing homes is run down and disorganized. Most of the nursing homes are still using paper and manual work to track medical records. Thus, there is a lack of trust and confidence between patients’ family and nursing homes. CarinMED solves this by providing a one-stop management platform not only to track patients’ records, but also to empower caregivers and nursing homes with productivity tools such as  customizable form builder, prescription reminder and reporting.

03 DOOR2DOOR HEALTHCARE
With the famous orange van around town, Door2Door Healthcare provides Home Healthcare And Medical Concierge services that are customized to patients’ needs to bring healthcare to their doorstep. The home healthcare services also include House Call Doctors, Private Nurses and Home Physiotherapy. D2DHelthcare.com is a simple, easy to use website that educates the users about the various services provided, thus allowing them to make better decision in booking specific services according to their needs.

Established since 1987, the Department of Primary Care Medicine has been leading primary care research and teaching in Malaysia and the region. As the oldest and the most prestigious public research university located in Kuala Lumpur, Malaysia, they have a strong team of academic staff who conducts behavioural and interventional research to solve real-world healthcare problems. The Department of Primary Care Medicine of the University of Malaya also train undergraduate and postgraduates students to become competent and compassionate family physicians.

05 HEALTH HACKATHON 2018
The Health Hackathon 2018 will be an educational journey as it aims to promote tailored mentoring so that participants can experience the best the industry has to offer and hence maximise the potential of their project. As a sign of the hackathon executive team’s commitment to nurture the collaborative culture and foster effective and meaningful innovation in healthcare, the branding is inspired by the game of Tetris. Thus, Health Hackathon 2018 will be anchored in collaboration and matching of different talents to achieve an objective. The ability to form a congruous group wins the game!
